The History of Halloween

How Halloween Was Born

Halloween (short for "All Hallows' Evening") is a traditional festival celebrated on October 31 every year, the evening before All Saints Day in Western Christianity. This is the beginning of the Allhallowtide â€“ the time in the liturgical year dedicated to remembering the dead, including saints, martyrs and all the faithful departed faithful. The traditional focus of Halloween revolves around the theme of using "humor and ridicule to confront the powers of death".

The popular view holds that many traditions of Halloween stem from Celtic harvest festivals that may have pagan origins, especially the Gaelic festival of Samhain, and that this festival was adopted by the Church. early Christianization. However, some academics suggest that Halloween developed independently of Samhain and has only Christian roots.

Source Of Halloween

Halloween is a festival that originated in Western countries. The word "Halloween" originates from the Christian church, which is a remnant of the festival of All Hallow Eve (holy feast). November 1st is the day when Christians show their sincerity before God.
However, in Celtic times, the harvest ended on October 31st, the Halloween festival called Samhain (sowing) which was November 1st. Because according to them: at this time the god of spring and summer no longer reigns and gives way to Death. When the spirits of the dead return to their loved ones' homes to ask for food and water, the corpses roam freely.

According to the ancients, on Samhain, the soul of the person who died in the previous year will find another body to start a new life in the next year. However, the living do not want the spirits of the dead to enter them, so on October 31 the villagers put out the fire in their houses to make the house cold and dull. The humans then disguised themselves as vampires or other monstrous forms to ward off body-seeking ghosts.

What People do on Halloween

In the atmosphere of Halloween is approaching, to increase the spooky and spooky level of your home, you can refer to the following ideas for decoration.
Paper Halloween decorations: You can skillfully shape paper items into monstrous shapes such as: Devil-faced pumpkins, ghosts, masquerade masks, ghost lanterns, ...
Decorate Halloween with glass and wall decals: In places such as walls, glass, windows, ... you can decorate with decal stickers with bold Halloween shapes such as: Decal of spider webs, bats, pumpkins corn, demon city, ...
Halloween decoration rope: You can make your own decorative wire from paper or plastic string, used to hang the words of Happy Halloween that you cut yourself or other decorative accessories on positions such as: Ceiling, wall , glasses, ... to make the festive atmosphere more lively.

Masquerade is considered the soul in Halloween parties, attendees can freely dress up according to their favorite characters and shapes, but especially to be as horror as possible. Many people believe that dressing up on Halloween also helps to scare away and intimidate ghosts, so as not to be "captured by the soul".

Some suggestions for Halloween costumes are simple but no less scary such as: Witch, faceless, undead, zombie, ...

To add more horror and drama to Halloween parties, it is definitely impossible to ignore popular games such as:

Trick Or Treat: Temporarily translated as "Give candy or be teased", to perform this game, children will wear any Halloween costume, then they will knock on doors from door to door to ask for candy and say "trick" or treat". If the host sends "treat" to them, which is usually cake and candy, they will receive thanks and wishes from the children and vice versa, they will be tricked by the children if they do not get the landlord's permission.

Apple Catching: Also one of the familiar games in Halloween. Apples will be stored in a bowl full of water, players will compete to see who can "catch" more apples in the water bowl without using their hands, that person will win or the longer the apple peels, the longer they will live.
Campfire tells ghost stories: On Halloween in the atmosphere full of ghosts and ghosts, making campfires telling ghost stories is one of the activities that make many people curious and interested. In addition, in the ancient Halloween festival, lighting a fire and playing and dancing around was also a form of good luck.
